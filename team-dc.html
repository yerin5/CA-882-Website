DRILL AND CEREMONIES

Drill and Ceremonies is a team within the CA-882 AFJROTC program. It is divided into two flights: Color Guard and Drill Team. Drill and Ceremonies cadets are expected to portray an enthusiastic learning attitude along with a disciplined mindset. Respect is fundamental, effort is always expected, and cadets will be recognized and awarded for their efforts. Extensive knowledge of drill will not only be expected, but will be enforced through inspections and written tests.

COLOR GUARD


Color Guard is one of two flights within the Drill and Ceremonies Squadron. If cadets choose to join this flight, they will learn the basics of flag and rifle movements and how to perform at events in four to five man teams (details). Color Guard focuses on synchronization and honoring the traditions and colors in events within the community. Cadets will perform specific scenarios such as, but not limited to, Presenting the Colors, Posting the Colors, Reveille and Retreat, and the Carrying of the Wreath.

HONOR GUARD


Honor Guard represents the pinnacle of Drill and Ceremonies within Unit CA-882 and stands as the symbol of excellence. This elite team is composed of the detachment’s top cadets, selected for exceptional proficiency in 30-Step, impeccable uniform standards, leadership, and dedication. Honor Guardsmen uphold and enforce the highest Drill and Ceremonies standards and represent CA-882 at performances, competitions, and traditional ceremonies. Selection is highly competitive and earned through a rigorous tryout process at the discretion of the Drill and Ceremonies Officers.

DRILL TEAM


The Drill Team is one of the two flights in the Drill and Ceremonies Squadron. Cadets must try out and be selected to join the Drill Team. Cadets here will focus solely on rifle movements, rifle spinning, and basic 30-step. Drill Team also requires physical acuteness as a degree of athleticism is required in order to perform all the proper movements.
